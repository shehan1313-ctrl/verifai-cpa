The Problem

AI sounds confident.
Even when it's wrong.

01

It misses law changes that happened after its training data

Tax law updates constantly — SECURE 2.0, TCJA provisions expiring, IRS inflation adjustments. LLMs are frozen in time and often don't know what they don't know.

"Yes, you can deduct home office expenses as a W-2 employee." — confidently wrong since 2017.

02

It gives population-level answers to your specific situation

Filing status, state, income mix, entity structure — all matter enormously. AI doesn't know what it doesn't know about you, and it won't tell you when to stop trusting it.

"It depends" repackaged as a clear yes — with no follow-up on the factors that actually determine the answer.

03

It invents deductions, thresholds, and rules that don't exist

Hallucination with complete confidence. A fabricated IRC section, a made-up deduction, a threshold that's been wrong for years. The model has no way to know it's wrong.

Citing a real-sounding code section that says something entirely different — or doesn't exist at all.
